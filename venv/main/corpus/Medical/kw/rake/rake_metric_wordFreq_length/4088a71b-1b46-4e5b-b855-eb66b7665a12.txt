Physical Exam: Non-contributory


CT scan to assess for worsening headache and visual problems followed by MRI (see images).
Unknown for this patient.  Treatment options may include:
• Medical - anticoagulation (heparin, coumadin), hydration, hemodilution
• Interventional radiology - endovascular thrombolysis, stenting
• Surgery - evacuate hematoma, etc.
Non-Contrast CT (NCT): High attenuation (density) consistent with a blood clot in right transverse sinus, sigmoid sinus, and extending to a thrombosis of the internal jugular vein .

Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI): High signal intensity lesion (clot) in same structures seen on T1W.
Note the distention of the involved venous sinuses by the thrombus.
