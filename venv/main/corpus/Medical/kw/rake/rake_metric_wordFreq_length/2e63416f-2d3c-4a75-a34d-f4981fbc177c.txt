Fracture femur
Patient's cerebral symptoms and MRI resolved spontaneously with no specific treatment.
• Midshaft fracture of femur.

• CT of brain in ER was negative (not shown)
• Routine brain MR shows multiple punctate foci on DWI involving the white matter of both cerebral hemispheres.
• ADC map shows corresponding low signal intensity foci - confirming cytotoxic edema

Tumor emboli from cardiac myxoma
16 y.o. girl in automobile accident.  She develops a decrease in level of consciousness 20 hours post accident.
NOTE:  PFO or other shunts are NOT required for CFE.  The fat droplets are malleable and can pass through an intact pulmonary capillary bed.  One key to suggest CFE is a normal CT in the face of MR changes.

» MR of cerebral fat embolism: may cause the "starfield pattern" of hyperintensities - seen on T2W and DWI - in the mid to deep white matter of both cerebral hemispheres.
