Obtunded woman with fever and visible drainage from left ear.
Patient underwent emergency drainage of the empyema.
Two days later she developed venous infarcts and underwent
decompressive craniotomy.
• Acute Mastoiditis, left
• Subdural Empyema with restricted diffusion, left
• Left transverse and sigmoid sinus thrombosis on MR venogram (MRV) • • Shows the "Empty Delta sign" on both contrast CT and MRI scans.
• Left temporal and occipital lobe hemorrhagic venous infarct.
Acute Mastoiditis, Transverse & Sigmoid sinus thrombosis, Subdural empyema, and hemorrhagic venous infarction
• Subdural hematoma
• Trauma
• Tumor
• Dural sinus thrombosis
47 year old woman with headache, fever, nausea and vomiting for two days prior to admission.
Outside CT scan revealed a subdural collection - thought to be an empyema, prompting referral to our hospital.  No attempt was made to open transverse and sigmoid sinus due to the delay in initial diagnosis and rapid clinical deterioration.
