19 year old woman, on birth control pills, presented to the ER with a headache following a seizure.
Due to the history of seizures and the findings on CT scan, the ER doctor admitted this patient with provisional diagnosis of brain tumor.

Venous infarcts are not typically diagnosed clinically.
If the radiologist does not suggest the diagnosis of venous thrombosis and/or infarction, then appropriate therapy may be delayed.
