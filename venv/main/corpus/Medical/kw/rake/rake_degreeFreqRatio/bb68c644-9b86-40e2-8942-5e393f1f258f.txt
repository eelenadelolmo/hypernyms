Bilateral enhancing masses within the carotid space (sheath) on both sides.  Masses have flow voids and splay open the proximal internal from external carotid arteries at the carotid bifurcation.
Bilateral Carotid body tumors
• Metastatic lymphadenopathy
• Vagal nerve Schwannomas 
• Thyroid cancer adenopathy
• Bilateral paragangliomas
38 year old male with history of minor trauma 1 year prior to presentation.  MRI cervical spine was done at outside hospital because of complaints of persistent neck pain.  Abnormality seen on outside MRI; and, a followup CT scan was recommended.
The presences of bilateral paragangliomas suggests a genetic predisposition:
• VHL - von Hippel-Lindau (autosomal dominant - AD)
• MEN2a / MEN2b - Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia Syndrome (AD)
• Neurofibromatosis type 1 - NF1, von Recklinghausen disease
• Familial Paraganglioma Syndrome  (succinate dehydrogenase mutation) http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK1548/?report=printable
