R cranial nerve VI palsy
Axial and coronal CT of the head and cervical spine demonstrating a R Occipital condyle fracture.
Occipital Condyle Fractures
Occipital condylar fracture Type I, II, or III
22yo M transported to the emergency department after a motorcycle accident. The head CT showed multiple intraparenchymal bleeds and small subarachnoid bleed. No other fractures of the cervical spine identified. Patient was monitored non-operatively by a hard collar. He developed a right cranial VI nerve palsy two days after the accident due to hemorrhage related to the fracture.
